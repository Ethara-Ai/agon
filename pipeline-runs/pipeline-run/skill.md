# Generalist Skill Registry

Canonical, task-agnostic competencies used to score candidates.

**skillset_version:** `v1-2026-06-15`
**status:** Draft, GENESIS mode

## Changelog

- 2026-06-15: GENESIS. Created the initial canonical set of 7 skills abstracted from the source guideline, each with a single weight 0 to 5. No project or task content is recorded in the vocabulary.

## Append-Only Governance

Existing skill IDs, names, scopes, and weights are permanent. Changes may only append a skill with the next free ID and its own weight, or sharpen wording without changing competency scope. Nothing is renamed, re-scoped, merged, split, reweighted, or deleted. Every append bumps the version and adds a changelog line.

## Canonical Skills

| ID | Name | Abstract description | Boundary | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | Instruction Reading | Decompose stated requirements into the distinct conditions and criteria that govern a judgment before making it. | Distinct from applying those criteria to evidence or from judging the underlying content. | 3 |
| S2 | Attention to Detail | Detect small but consequential visual or textual features that determine a judgment. | Distinct from deciding how strongly the observed evidence supports a conclusion. | 4 |
| S3 | Calibrated Judgment | Match the strength and confidence of a conclusion to the clarity and completeness of the available evidence, and recognize when evidence is too thin to decide at all. | Distinct from judging whether the evidence is relevant, and from recognizing that the source material itself is unfit to judge. | 5 |
| S4 | Objectivity | Base a conclusion only on the evidence genuinely relevant to it, excluding incidental signals, surface resemblance, and unsupported inference. | Distinct from confidence calibration when the relevant evidence is incomplete. | 5 |
| S5 | Difference Detection | Identify the specific correspondences and conflicts between two observations, such as matching, added, missing, or differing features. | Distinct from ranking alternatives or judging their overall quality. | 5 |
| S6 | Source Validity Judgment | Recognize when the source material itself is unfit for the intended judgment, separate from any difficulty in reaching that judgment. | Distinct from being unable to decide a well-formed case, which is a calibration outcome. | 3 |
| S7 | Clear Writing | Express a conclusion and the specific evidence supporting it concisely, concretely, and unambiguously. | Distinct from the correctness of the conclusion being explained. | 4 |
